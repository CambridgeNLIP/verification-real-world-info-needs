Evidence tiebreaking task

Welcome to this evidence tiebreaking task. You must select a minimal set of sentences from a short passage from Wikipedia that provide evidence to say whether a claim is true or false.

This task follows from the evidence finding task. Every HIT in this task has at least 1 annotation indicating that it contains evidence. It is your job to act as a tiebreaker and confirm whether this passage has evidence in it or not. The format, guidelines and keyboard shortcuts are the same as the previous task and are reiterated below:

Why are we collecting this data and what are we looking for?

Our research group is collecting data to evaluate how computers can find and understand written information. While search engines are very good at finding related information, they struggle with finding evidence that persuades someone that a claim is true or false. In this HIT, the claims you are labelling are search queries people have made and the page you are labelling has a good chance of containing information that indicates the claim is true or false. Your job is to select the sentences which indicate whether the claim is true or false so that we can compare whether human intuition can be captured by these artificial intelligence systems.

Guidelines

  • Only select sentences that are necessary for you to form a judgement (either by combining some sentences together or a set of sentences that each individually substantiate the claim).
  • A simple way to check whether a sentence is 'evidence' is to ask yourself "without this sentence, would my opinion whether the claim is true or false change?". If yes, the sentence would be evidence. If no, then do not include the sentence
  • Sentences that do not provide evidence to say whether a claim is true or false should not be selected
  • Some sentences have more than one sentence that supports the claim equally well. Select all sentences that are evidence.
  • You must only the information from the text snippets we provide to inform your decision. You MUST NOT use any external resources such as a search engine or external Wikipedia articles. You are allowed to use general knowledge or common sense.
  • The page and section title are to provide you with context. But should not be part of the evidence you use to form your decision. Only make your decision from the sentences in the document that you can highlight.
  • If a sentence refers to an object using a pronoun (it, he, she, they, etc) or anaphora (the album, the car, etc.), you must also select evidence that that describes what the pronoun refers to (see example 2). If the meaning of the pronoun in a sentence cannot be resolved from selecting an extra sentence, it is not evidence.

HIT Review Policy

We will continuously monitor annotation quality through manually reviewing your submissions and provide feedback where appropriate. Where a high number of errors are caused by 'rushing', we may reject some of these HITs rather than providing feedback. Workers who have a higher than average error rate, faster than average submission times, unusual annotation patterns, or disagree with other workers may be more likely to be manually reviewed.

To ensure diversity and quality of the data, and minimize worker fatigue, we may give accounts with very high submission rates a temporary time-out.

Step-by-step annotation process

  1. Read the claim.
  2. Read each sentence from the Wikipedia passage and label it as follows:
    • If the sentence provides evidence to say the claim is true, select T.
    • If the sentence provides evidence to say the claim is false, select F.
    • The default option is - where the sentence is not evidence. There will be some cases where no evidence can be found in the passage.
    Pay special attention to the Wikipedia page or section title as it might be necessary in order to understand the relationship between the sentences in the passage and the claim.
  3. Once you have read the entire page and have finished selecting evidence. Press Next
    • If you selected evidence, you will be asked decide whether the claim is true or false and asked a couple of questions about your decision.
    • If there isn't enough information for you to be certain but the evidence selected indicates that it is likely that the claim is true or false,
      select I need to would need to find more evidence to be sure
    • If you could find no information to say whether the claim is true or false, you will be asked to record whether the passage is relevant.
      This means the passage is discussing the information in the claim, but does not contain strong evidence that supports or refutes the claim.
  4. After you have answered the questions, click Submit to record your annotations and complete the HIT

false
because of the following evidence from the Wikipedia page titled La Coupe des Mousquetaires:
La Coupe des Mousquetaires is the trophy awarded to the winner of the Men's Singles competition at the French Open.
The winner does not get to keep the actual trophy; a new replica is produced every year.
The first sentence is used to give context. As the Wikipedia page is about La Coupe des Mousquetaires, the first sentence states that this is awarded to the winner of the french open. Without this first sentence, the second sentence would not be sufficient.

is
false
because of the following evidence from the Wikipedia page titled Seashell resonance:
There is a popular folk myth that if one holds a seashell—specifically, most often, a conch shell—to one's ear, one can hear the sound of the ocean.
The rushing sound that one hears is in fact the noise of the surrounding environment, resonating within the cavity of the shell.

There are some cases where there may be conflicting information. In this case, the first sentence describes the normal behavior and the second sentence provides some exceptions that attack, but do not fully refute, the first sentence.

You can get a tattoo as an 18 year old.
is
true
because of the following evidence from the Wikipedia page titled Legal status of tatooing in the United States :
However, all 50 states the District of Columbia have statutory laws requiring a person receiving a tattoo be at least 18 years old.
Most states permit a person under the age of 18 to receive a tattoo with permission of a parent or guardian.

Vanilla sugar is made of sugar and vanilla beans or sugar mixed with vanilla extract
